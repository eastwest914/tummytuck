FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about tummy tuck surgery and abdominoplasty at our Hoboken, NJ practice.

Am I a good candidate for a tummy tuck?
Good candidates are generally in good overall health, at a stable weight, and dealing with loose abdominal skin or separated muscles that diet and exercise haven't resolved. A consultation with our team will confirm whether abdominoplasty is right for you.
What's the difference between a mini and a full tummy tuck?
A mini tummy tuck targets only the lower abdomen with a shorter incision and quicker recovery, while a full (traditional) tummy tuck addresses the entire abdominal area, including muscle repair above and below the navel.
How long is the recovery after abdominoplasty surgery?
Most patients take about two to three weeks off work and several more weeks before resuming strenuous activity. We'll give you a personalized recovery timeline and follow-up schedule during your consultation.
Will a tummy tuck remove stretch marks?
A tummy tuck removes excess skin, which can eliminate stretch marks located in that area — particularly below the belly button. Stretch marks on remaining skin may still be visible, and we'll discuss realistic expectations with you.
Can a tummy tuck be combined with other procedures?
Yes. Many patients combine abdominoplasty with liposuction or breast procedures as part of a mommy makeover. We'll help determine the safest, most effective combination for your goals during your consultation.
